# Premium Value Analysis: Effira Energy (Sweden)

**Date**: February 25, 2026
**Last updated**: February 27, 2026
**Scope**: Effira Energy AB (Sweden) — public website, recorded webinar, in-person seminar deck, internal strategy materials
**Prepared by**: External consultant, supported by structured value framework analysis

---

## Executive Summary

Effira Energy's product OPTi is an AI-based heat pump and hot water optimizer, sold to Swedish homeowners as a subscription-installed service. The strongest premium proposition is not just lower bills — it is **lower bills without comfort risk and without requiring the homeowner to actively manage anything**.

The value analysis confirms a fingerprint of **Reassurance → Smart → Wellbeing**. This is internally consistent across all source materials. The strategic gap is that Effira's current public-facing channels — particularly the website and webinar — are calibrated for the Smart-primary buyer, while the majority of the addressable market is Reassurance- and Wellbeing-primary.

The segmentation and funnel analysis that follows translates this into a prioritized go-to-market view: who to approach first, with what message, through which channel, and on what timeline.

## Part 1: Analysis of Source Materials

### 1.1 Website (effiraenergy.com/se/)

The website is the primary cold-prospect touchpoint and sets the frame for most first impressions of OPTi.

**What it leads with**: Savings. The homepage opens with a 3,000–15,000 kr/year headline and an interactive savings calculator. The calculator is the primary conversion mechanism — it invites the user to quantify their potential gain before anything else is established.

**What it does well**:
- The savings range is credible and broad enough to include most heat pump households in SE3/SE4
- Reassurance is well-represented in volume: 8 of 20 sampled high-salience messages are Reassurance-coded (guarantee, compatibility, warranty-safe, no need to do anything yourself, professional installation)
- "Utan att kompromissa med värmen" is present — comfort preservation is stated, if lightly

**What it does less well**:
- The website leads with Smart and positions Reassurance as supporting rather than leading — the inverse of the fingerprint
- Wellbeing is minimal: two lines in 20 sampled messages, neither functioning as a premium claim
- The most important Reassurance claim in the entire corpus — the comfort protection mechanism (OPTi only cuts heating when stored thermal mass is sufficient, making comfort failure structurally impossible) — does not appear anywhere on the website
- "Allt sker inom komfortens ramar" exists in the Steninge deck but has never been brought to the website
- For the Reassurance-primary buyer, the savings-first entry sequence triggers a specific anxiety: *if this system is optimizing my heating, it might make my home cold.* That anxiety is raised before it is resolved.

**Value distribution (sampled 20 messages)**:

| Value | Count | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Reassurance | 8 | 40% |
| Smart | 5 | 25% |
| Wellbeing | 2 | 10% |
| Citizenship | 2 | 10% |
| Unclassified (feature/spec) | 2 | 10% |
| Joy / Recognition / Affirmation / Connection | 0 | 0% |

**Assessment**: A well-constructed Optimizer funnel. Not yet a Delegator or Comfort Keeper funnel.

### 1.2 Steninge Energiseminarium (Feb 10, 2026)

A face-to-face energy seminar with a self-selected, warm audience. The highest-trust format in Effira's current channel mix.

**Value framing stated on the product slide**: "Kontroll | Komfort | Lägre elkostnader"

**Key claims and what they tell us**:

- **"Allt sker inom komfortens ramar"** — All optimization happens within the boundaries of comfort. This is the strongest single claim across all Effira materials. Comfort is framed as the operating constraint, not just a benefit. This is a structural guarantee, not a promise. It should be a homepage hero claim.

- **15× battery comparison** — "Det är 15 gånger dyrare att investera i batterier jämfört med att investera i smart styrning av värmepumpen." Decisive and memorable. The clearest category-positioning argument in the corpus — makes OPTi's value immediately legible against the most visible alternative.

- **Effect tariff landscape** — 16–160 kr/kW across grid operators; all Sweden mandated by 2027-01-01. Strong context for the Optimizer and Delegator who have recently seen their bill structure change.

- **Solar OPTi case** — +40% self-consumption = +1,600 kWh = ~2,500 kr/year. A specific, quantified value claim for the solar owner segment.

- **"Värdestegring av bostaden"** — Home value appreciation listed as an economic benefit. Extends Smart framing beyond running cost to asset value.

- **Citizenship close** — Nuclear power station slide: "Ett litet beslut från dig som bidrar till den stora samhällsnyttan." Correctly sequenced *after* the economic and comfort proof, not before. The right tone and position for Citizenship.

- **Closing slide** — Warm sunny living room, no text. Effira's chosen emotional endpoint is domestic comfort, not savings or technology.

**Value sequence (Steninge)**: Kontroll → Komfort → Ekonomi → Samhällsnytta

**Value distribution (sampled 10 high-salience claims)**:

|---|---|---|

| Wellbeing | 3 | 30% |

**Assessment**: The most value-aligned piece of content in the corpus. Wellbeing is significantly stronger here than on the website — comfort is not a supporting claim but the structural operating constraint. Smart is anchored in comparative proof (15× battery comparison, quantified solar case, payback framing) rather than abstract claims. Reassurance is present but implicit — carried by the format itself and by "allt sker inom komfortens ramar" — rather than foregrounded as a distinct message.

**Format assessment**: The in-person format pre-establishes trust — attendance itself is a commitment signal. Q&A resolves objections in real time. This is the optimal format for the Reassurance- and Wellbeing-primary buyer. The Steninge deck is Effira's best-performing piece of content in terms of value alignment. The gap is that it only reaches warm, already-interested audiences.

### 1.3 Recorded Webinar (Feb 2026)

A 45-minute recorded webinar with Stefan Lundström (Effira energy expert) and host William. ~150–200 live attendees. Publicly available.

**Strongest claims in the corpus**:

- **"Pågår bakom kulisserna utan att jag egentligen behöver bry mig"** — the purest Reassurance statement in all Effira materials. Spoken from personal use by the presenter. It is buried mid-webinar and has not been brought into any written or structured messaging.

- **"Det kommer inte att bli för kallt — för då stängs det inte av"** — the comfort protection mechanism explained for the first time in consumer language. The system only acts on heating when stored thermal energy is sufficient. This is a design guarantee, not a commercial promise. It is the single most defensible Reassurance claim available to Effira and it appears as a technical footnote.

- **"Vi lever precis som vanligt. Vi har 22 grader i huset. Familjen är happy med det."** — "22 grader" is used repeatedly as a concrete comfort anchor. A lived, specific, believable Wellbeing claim.

- **Payback framing**: OPTi costs ~5,000 kr and saves ~9–10,000 kr/year. Payback ~8 months vs. ~7 years for a home battery. Reinforces the 15× argument from Steninge with a time dimension.

**Value ordering (spoken)**: Komfort → Kontroll → Lägre elkostnader — the correct sequence for a Reassurance-primary fingerprint. Comfort comes first in the spoken presentation, which is right, but is then buried under 15 minutes of market education before the audience hears it.

**Where the jury is out — three format tensions**:

**Tension 1 — Market education before relief**: The webinar opens with effect tariff explanations, quarterly price volatility, solar subsidy removal, and grid complexity. For the Reassurance buyer — whose entire need is to have this complexity removed from their life — this sequence activates the exact anxiety OPTi is supposed to resolve. The Reassurance buyer needs to hear "we handle this for you" before being shown how complex "this" is.

**Tension 2 — Format contradicts the product promise**: A 45-minute webinar implicitly communicates that this category requires active engagement. OPTi's core promise is "pågår bakom kulisserna utan att jag behöver bry mig." A 45-minute commitment to understand the product before purchase signals ongoing management, not delegation. The format self-selects for the Optimizer and filters out the Delegator.

**Tension 3 — "Winner" identity framing**: The closing line — "Att sitta still blir man inte en vinnare på den nya elmarknaden" — positions the buyer as a strategic market actor. The Reassurance buyer does not identify as a market actor. They want to be told: this has been handled. You don't need to win. We'll take care of it.

**Assessment**: The webinar works well for the Optimizer segment. For the Delegator and Comfort Keeper, the format and structure are a mismatch with the value proposition. The content is largely right; the structure and framing need rethinking for a broader audience.

### 1.4 Category Failure Mode Analysis (from internal strategy deck, Jan–Feb 2026)

The internal deck frames OPTi explicitly as a response to 15 years of smart energy product failure. This is the most strategically useful framing in any Effira material — it explains not just what OPTi does, but *why the category has failed to reach mainstream adoption and how OPTi is structurally different*.

| Category failure mode | OPTi response | Value implication |
|---|---|---|
| The user must be smart and engaged to benefit | Product is smart — minimal user input required | Reassurance: removes cognitive burden |
| High price point (solar, batteries: 50,000–100,000 SEK) | Low installation cost, high marginal return | Smart: accessible ROI |
| Few products deliver promised savings | Savings guarantee + proof-backed claims | Reassurance: credibility infrastructure |
| Products address only a small part of overall consumption | Addresses ~75% of home energy use | Smart: material impact |
| Control is often only oversight, not real control | Real-time control capability | Smart: genuine agency |
| Most products include a trade-off between comfort and convenience | No trade-off — comfort is the operating constraint | Wellbeing: structural, not promised |
| Targets "the smartification nerd" | Designed for the mainstream comfort-and-savings buyer | Reassurance + Wellbeing: broadened target |
| Self-installation standard (cords, hassle) | Swedish engineering with professional installation | Reassurance: reduced setup friction |

**What this tells us**: The internal deck confirms that Effira has identified the same three premium drivers that the external value analysis reaches independently — Reassurance, Smart, and Wellbeing — and has designed OPTi's product and commercial model as a direct counter to each category failure mode that blocked them. The strategic logic is sound. The implementation gap is that this clarity has not yet translated consistently into the public-facing message architecture.

This analysis uses an 8-value premium consumer framework. Each value is classified across three dimensions:

- **Extrinsic / Intrinsic**: Is the value delivered through product performance and measurable outcomes, or felt directly as a lived experience?
- **Self / Other**: Does the value primarily benefit the individual or extend to others?
- **Active / Reactive**: Does the consumer use the product to generate value, or does the product act on the consumer?

| Affirmation | Identity reinforcement — the product reflects who I am |
| Joy | Pleasure, delight, and sensory satisfaction |
| Wellbeing | Comfort, warmth, health, and physical ease |
| Smart | Better decisions, outcomes, savings, and optimization |
| Reassurance | Trust, reliability, safety, risk reduction, and a sense of control — the product acts so the consumer doesn't have to |
| Connection | Relationships, family, belonging, and shared experience |

### 2.3 Proposed Premium Fingerprint

#### Reassurance (Core)

The category's dominant failure modes — user cognitive burden, undelivered savings, equipment risk, comfort trade-offs — all map directly onto Reassurance as the key unmet need. Effira's product design responds to each: professional installation, savings guarantee, warranty-safe operation, and a comfort protection mechanism that makes comfort failure structurally impossible rather than just promised. "Pågår bakom kulisserna" (item 25) and "det kommer inte att bli för kallt" (item 26) are the purest expressions of this. Both are currently buried; neither is a headline claim.

**Premium mechanism**: Consumers pay more when they trust the system to reduce risk and optimize safely on their behalf — giving them a sense of control without the burden of active management. The value is not just that the system works; it is that the consumer no longer has to think about whether it will.

#### Smart (Primary supporting driver)

Savings and optimization performance are necessary to enter the premium set — the category exists to improve outcomes. The 15× battery comparison (item 30) and 8-month payback (item 28) are the strongest Smart conversion arguments in the corpus: they are specific, comparative, and memorable. The 75% energy coverage claim (items 21, 23) makes the scope of optimization credible. App visibility reinforces the sense of informed agency.

**Premium mechanism**: Consumers pay more when they believe the optimization quality is genuinely superior and independently verifiable.

#### Wellbeing (Primary supporting driver)

Savings that reduce comfort tend to fail adoption. The category norm is to pair cost reduction with comfort preservation language, but Effira has a stronger claim available: "allt sker inom komfortens ramar" (item 29) positions comfort not as a benefit but as the operating constraint. This is a structurally different and more powerful claim — it is not "we try not to make you cold," it is "the system will not act unless your comfort is already secured." The warm living room closing image in Steninge (item 33) confirms that domestic comfort is Effira's intended emotional endpoint.

**Premium mechanism**: Consumers pay more when they are confident that optimization will preserve or improve felt home comfort, not compromise it.

#### Citizenship (Supporting narrative)

Useful for brand differentiation and category legitimacy — grid balancing, the energy transition, and community contribution are real and genuine. But it is not a primary WTP driver for most homeowners in this category. The Steninge deck sequences it correctly: after economic and comfort proof, as a closing resonance, not as a lead claim. That sequencing should be followed in all channels.

### 2.6 Competitive Value Positioning

#### The competitive landscape

Four products appear in the Swedish heat pump optimization category alongside OPTi: Ngenic Tune, Thermia Smart Price Adaption, NIBE myUplink, and HeatAdapt. Each overlaps with OPTi on the Smart dimension — automatic spot-price optimization is effectively the category entry claim, present in all products. The differences emerge on Reassurance and Wellbeing.

#### Where OPTi is differentiated

**Comfort protection mechanism — unique in the category.** No competitor has a specific, named, verifiable mechanism that guarantees comfort is never sacrificed. HeatAdapt's "without compromising indoor comfort" is the closest, but it is a positioning promise, not a design mechanism. OPTi's claim — the system only acts when stored thermal mass is sufficient, making comfort failure structurally impossible — is a different class of claim. It can be explained in one sentence, verified by a customer, and is difficult to copy without a genuine product change.

**Brand-agnostic compatibility — structural advantage over OEM-locked competitors.** Thermia and NIBE optimize only their own heat pumps. A household with a NIBE pump can use NIBE myUplink; a household with a Thermia pump can use Thermia Smart Price Adaption; a household with anything else cannot. OPTi's 94–95% compatibility across brands means it is the only real option for the majority of Swedish heat pump owners who did not buy Thermia or NIBE — and a potentially better option even for those who did, given the integrated hot water coverage.

**Hot water as part of the system — coverage others lack.** Most category competitors focus on space heating optimization. OPTi addresses heating and hot water together (~75% of total home energy consumption). For frånluft households in particular — where hot water is the dominant cost — competitors that address heating only are solving a fraction of the problem.

**Professional installation — a Reassurance differentiator vs. DIY alternatives.** Ngenic Tune and similar smart home energy products follow the category norm of self-installation. For CARE-dominant segments (Delegator, Comfort Keeper), DIY installation is an immediate friction point — it signals ongoing complexity and creates setup anxiety. OPTi's professional, included installation removes this barrier entirely.

---

#### Where OPTi is at parity or at risk

**Spot-price optimization is category table stakes.** All competitors offer some form of automatic spot-price adaptation. OPTi cannot win on this claim alone — it needs to win on what happens *around* the optimization (comfort certainty, coverage breadth, installation quality).

**OEM-tied competitors have embedded distribution.** Thermia and NIBE reach their customers through the heat pump purchase relationship — the installer who sold the pump also activates the optimization. OPTi has to build this installer relationship from scratch. The upside is that OPTi is not constrained to one brand; the challenge is that brand-tied competitors may activate the installer channel more easily within their ecosystem.

**HeatAdapt is the most directly competing positioning.** Of the four, HeatAdapt is the only one to explicitly pair energy savings with comfort preservation — the same claim territory as OPTi. If HeatAdapt develops a mechanistic comfort guarantee or broadens its compatibility, the differentiation narrows. This is the competitor to monitor most closely.

## Part 3: Segmentation — A Suggested Framing

*The frameworks below represent my suggested segmentation for Effira, combining behavioral targeting logic with value-based messaging. These are working hypotheses to trigger strategic thinking — not validated customer research. Effira should test and refine them against sales data, customer interviews, and channel experience.*

---

### 3.1 Two Lenses, One Test

Segmentation for a product like OPTi requires two complementary frameworks working together:

- **Behavioral segments** answer the question: *who are these people and where can I find them?* They group buyers by identifiable life situation, household type, or product setup. They are the targeting unit — the brief that goes to media or the installer.
- **Value-based archetypes** answer the question: *what do they need to feel before they will act?* They group buyers by motivational profile. They are the messaging brief — what to lead with, what objection to resolve first, what proof claim converts.

**The test that matters most is actionability.** A segment is only useful if you can find the people in it. A beautifully described motivational profile that cannot be targeted on any media platform, handed to an installer, or reached through search is an intellectual exercise. The segments below are ordered by how identifiable and reachable they are in practice.

---

### 3.2 B2C Segments

#### Segment 1: Families with Children / Comfort-Driven — *the priority segment*

**Who they are**: Households with children at home, typically aged 35–50, dual income, villa or larger apartment in SE3/SE4. They own a heat pump — installed in the last 5–10 years — and carry high household energy costs. Time is their scarcest resource. Energy is not something they want to think about; it is something they want handled.

**Why this is the most important segment**: Families with children are directly and precisely identifiable. Meta and Facebook allow targeting by life stage (parent of child under 18), property type, and geography — this is not a broad demographic sweep, it is a defined population. The combination of high household energy consumption, time pressure, and genuine comfort priority (children in cold rooms is not acceptable) makes the OPTi value proposition a near-perfect fit. The message writes itself: warm home, no work, lower bills.

**How to find them**:
- Meta/Facebook: Parents aged 35–50, homeowners, SE3/SE4 postcodes
- Google: Family energy content, parent community sites, school catchment area targeting
- Installer channel: VVS installers who service family-home-dense suburbs are the direct route

**Value archetype**: Delegator (Reassurance-primary). They want to hand over control and stop thinking about it. Comfort is non-negotiable — the comfort protection mechanism is the decisive claim. "Trygg värme och varmvatten — helt automatiskt" is the right headline.

**What converts them**: A short testimonial video from a family describing their experience: "vi installerade det och glömde bort det — men räkningarna sjönk." Professional installation, no DIY, no app-management requirement. The comfort guarantee must be stated plainly.

**What creates friction**: Any implication that the household should monitor, adjust, or engage with the system. Market complexity language (tariffs, algorithms, spot prices) before comfort reassurance has been established.

---

#### Segment 2: Spot-Price Actives (Tibber / rörligt pris users)

**Who they are**: Homeowners already on variable spot-price contracts who have actively engaged with energy pricing — through Tibber, their utility's app, or a home automation setup. They have chosen to be exposed to hourly price variation because they believe they can benefit from it. They are already optimizing, manually or through basic tools, and are looking for something better.

**How to find them**:
- Tibber app community and Facebook groups (self-identified energy community)
- Search intent: "optimera timpris," "värmepump timpris," "automatisk spotpris styrning"
- Energy forums (Flashback energi/teknik threads, Reddit energy communities)
- Potential Tibber partnership: co-marketing to existing user base

**Value archetype**: Optimizer (Smart-primary). They want to understand the algorithm, see granular savings data, and verify performance before committing. They have found limitations in what they currently do and are open to upgrading.

**What converts them**: Proof. The 15× battery comparison, 8-month payback, and savings calculator with adjustable inputs. Transparency about how OPTi's optimization logic differs from basic spot-price steering. Access to real performance data from comparable heating setups. They will find the one critical review — preempt it.

**What creates friction**: Vague savings claims. "AI" framing without explanation. Any perception that OPTi offers only monitoring rather than genuine real-time control.

---

#### Segment 3: Ground Source Heat Pump Owners (Bergvärme)

**Who they are**: Homeowners who invested in a bergvärme installation — typically a higher upfront cost than luft/vatten — and regard themselves as having made a serious, considered decision about their home energy system. They tend to be 45–65, own larger properties, and take pride in having the technically superior solution. They are interested in getting maximum return from a system they have already invested in heavily.

**How to find them**:
- Search: "bergvärme optimering," "sänk elkostnad bergvärme," specific bergvärme brand communities
- VVS installer channel: bergvärme servicing engineers are the direct relationship
- NIBE, Bosch, IVT owner communities online

**Value archetype**: Optimizer (Smart-primary). They want the best performance from their existing investment, verified ROI, and peer credibility for the decision.

**What converts them**: Framing OPTi as the software upgrade their hardware was always capable of — making a high-quality system perform at its ceiling. Savings data from comparable bergvärme installations is the most persuasive proof format.

**What creates friction**: Anything that implies OPTi is a workaround for an underperforming system. They chose bergvärme precisely because they wanted the best.

---

#### Segment 4: Solar Owners

**Who they are**: Homeowners with residential solar installations, now facing a specific and recent financial problem: the 60-öre/kWh solar export subsidy has been materially reduced. They invested in solar partly on the basis of export compensation that no longer exists at the same level. They are already comfortable with energy investment decisions, already think in system terms, and are actively looking for ways to recapture the ROI their calculations assumed.

**How to find them**:
- Solar installer databases and referral programs (most direct and highest-quality route)
- Facebook groups for Swedish solar owners
- Search: "solceller värmepump integration," "optimera solceller," "60 öre borttaget"
- Content hook: "Vad gör du nu när 60-öressubventionen är borta?" — high organic search intent

**Value archetype**: Solar Owner (Smart-primary, specific trigger). They are not buying generic heat pump optimization — they are buying the solution to a specific problem they have already identified.

**What converts them**: A clear articulation of the solar self-consumption case: OPTi shifts heat pump load to peak solar production hours, increasing self-consumption by ~40% = +1,600 kWh = ~2,500 kr/year. Modeled against their actual panel capacity. This is a replacement ROI calculation, not a generic savings pitch.

**What creates friction**: Generic messaging. This segment will immediately recognize when a product is positioned broadly and assume the solar integration is superficial.

---

#### Segment 5: Radhus and Townhouse Owners

**Who they are**: Owners of radhus or smaller attached villas, often with frånluft heat pumps where warm water is the dominant energy load (not just space heating). Many are in newer residential developments. Their energy situation is often misunderstood by generic heat pump optimization products that focus only on heating — for them, hot water is the larger cost.

**How to find them**:
- Property type targeting: radhus areas in SE3/SE4 are geographically clustered and addressable by postcode
- VVS installer channel: frånluft installers and servicers serve this housing type specifically
- Local advertising in radhus-dense suburban areas

**Value archetype**: Comfort Keeper and Delegator. The household management orientation is strong — warmth and hot water availability are baseline standards, not negotiable. The OPTi message for this segment must address hot water specifically: the claim that OPTi manages both heating and hot water as a combined system is the product differentiator.

**What converts them**: "Frånluft handlar om varmvatten — och OPTi hanterar det." The comfort + automation combination. An installer who specifically understands frånluft systems.

**What creates friction**: Messaging that only addresses space heating. A product that is perceived as solving the wrong problem.

---

#### Segment 6: Older Villa Owners (Trygghetskop)

**Who they are**: Homeowners aged 60+, long-term owners of a free-standing villa, often with a heat pump installed at least 5 years ago. Fixed income or approaching it. High risk aversion — they are not early adopters of technology and have seen smart home products come and go. Their primary motivation is stability and predictability: they want to know that things will continue to work as they do now, while spending a little less.

**How to find them**:
- Meta/Facebook: Age 60+, homeowners, SE3/SE4, long-term property ownership signals
- Local channels: regional press, community boards, neighborhood associations
- Installer channel: this segment trusts a known local tradesperson above all brand communication

**Value archetype**: Comfort Keeper (Wellbeing + Reassurance). Comfort is the absolute non-negotiable. The fear of smart systems making things worse is real and must be addressed directly and simply.

**What converts them**: A trusted installer's recommendation, stated plainly. "Spara pengar utan att ändra hur du lever." The comfort protection mechanism explained in one sentence, not a technical description. Human support contact during onboarding.

**What creates friction**: Any technology-forward framing. App engagement requirements. Complexity signals of any kind.

---

#### Segment 7: Effect Tariff-Affected Households

**Who they are**: Households who have recently seen their electricity bill structure change due to new effect tariffs (effektavgifter) — charging based on peak consumption rather than volume alone. SE3/SE4 zone, variable tariff. They may not have understood or anticipated this change, and are now looking for a solution to a bill they did not expect.

**How to find them**:
- Search intent: "effektavgift," "undvika effekttopp," "sänka effekttopp värmepump" — high-intent, problem-aware
- SEO is the primary channel; this is a search-driven segment
- Google Display targeting to SE3/SE4 postcode areas following tariff communications

**Value archetype**: Delegator (Reassurance-primary). The anxiety is about unpredictable costs. The OPTi hot water peak-shaving capability — loading warm water at night to avoid daytime peak charges — is the specific product proof for this segment.

**What converts them**: Specific explanation of how OPTi addresses peak tariffs, not just spot price optimization. "Slipp dyra effekttoppar — ladda varmvatten på natten." The message should be reactive to a known, named problem.

**What creates friction**: Generic savings messaging. They have a specific problem; they need a specific solution.

## Part 4: The Funnel — Which Segment First?

### 4.1 Market Sizing: What the Numbers Mean

**TAM — Total Addressable Market: 830,000–930,000 homes**

The TAM is defined as Swedish homes with a relevant heat pump type: bergvärme (ground source), luft/vatten (air-to-water), or frånluft (exhaust air). These are the heat pump categories OPTi is compatible with and where the system can deliver meaningful optimization. This is a straightforward count — how many homes in Sweden could theoretically use this product if all other conditions were met?

**SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market: 390,000–430,000 homes**

The SAM applies three filters to the TAM:
- **Geography**: Homes in SE3 and SE4 electricity price zones, where hourly spot prices are highest and most volatile. Outside these zones (SE1, SE2), the financial case for optimization weakens.
- **Tariff structure**: Homes on variable/spot-price contracts (rörligt pris). Fixed-price contracts eliminate the hourly price arbitrage that OPTi exploits.
- **Technical compatibility**: Retrofit-compatible heat pump installations (excluding very new units where optimization may already be built in or very old units where integration is not feasible).

The SAM calculation: 0.70 (SE3/SE4 geography) × 0.74 (variable tariff share) × 0.90 (retrofit compatibility) × TAM midpoint ≈ 390K–430K homes.

This is still a large and tractable number. Every home in this pool has the right heating setup, the right tariff structure, and the right geography for OPTi to deliver value.

**SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market: This is where choices begin**

The internal deck estimates 16,000–17,000 customers by 2028 — approximately 1% of SAM through direct B2C and ~3% through the installer channel. These figures are illustrative, not derived from a bottoms-up capacity model. The honest question to ask about SOM is not "what percentage of SAM can we reach?" but **"what can this company actually execute in this time frame?"**

SOM is constrained by four factors that have nothing to do with the size of the market:

**1. Installer channel activation capacity**
The installer channel is the highest-leverage route to the majority of the SAM (CARE-dominant segments). But activating installers requires training, tooling, and account management. If Effira can onboard and support 30 active VVS installers in SE3/SE4 by the end of 2026, and each installer does 8–10 OPTi installations per month, that generates ~3,000 installs/year from the installer channel alone. Scaling to 16K customers by end-2028 implies needing ~5,300 installs per year — which requires either ~60–65 active installers at 8/month throughput, or higher direct B2C volume to compensate.

**2. Direct sales and onboarding throughput**
How many direct B2C customers can Effira onboard, support through installation, and retain without service quality degrading? This is a function of operational capacity: customer service staffing, installation partner network, and digital self-service capability. Scaling before operations are ready creates churn from bad first experiences — which, for a Reassurance-primary product, is particularly damaging.

**3. Marketing reach and budget**
The Optimizer segment is reachable through low-cost digital channels (search, forums, webinar). The Delegator and Comfort Keeper require different channels (Meta targeting, in-person events, installer network) that require either paid media budget or relationship-building time. The SOM in the near term is bounded by what marketing activity can actually be funded and executed.

**4. Brand awareness baseline**
Effira is not yet a recognized consumer brand in Swedish homes. The awareness-building phase is not instantaneous. Even with good messaging, the funnel from awareness to consideration to trial to purchase takes months. The realistic SOM for Year 1 reflects the output of a brand-building effort that is still early-stage.

**A working bottoms-up SOM model**:

| Time horizon | Installer channel (est.) | Direct B2C | Cumulative customers |
|---|---|---|---|
| End 2026 | ~20 active installers × 6 installs/month × 6 months ≈ 720 | ~600 direct | ~1,300 |
| End 2027 | ~45 active installers × 8 installs/month × 12 months ≈ 4,320 | ~1,800 direct | ~7,400 |
| End 2028 | ~65 active installers × 10 installs/month × 12 months ≈ 7,800 | ~2,500 direct | ~17,700 |

This model reaches the internal 16K–17K target — but only if installer activation goes to plan and direct B2C conversion improves materially. Both assumptions require active commercial investment, not just organic growth.

### 4.3 Phase 1: 3–6 Months — Establish and Prove

**Priority segments**: The Optimizer (primary), first VVS installer activations (Delegator pipeline)

**Why the Optimizer first**: This is not because the Optimizer is the most important segment — they are not, by volume. It is because the Optimizer is reachable now, with existing materials, through channels Effira already operates. Early Optimizer customers generate two things the other segments depend on: revenue and proof. Without a growing base of real savings data, real comfort testimonials, and real performance cases, the Delegator and Comfort Keeper conversion arguments lack the credibility they require.

**Parallel move — activate the installer channel**: The Optimizer-first digital funnel buys time to build the installer channel. Identifying and onboarding the first 15–20 active VVS partners in SE3/SE4 is the most important single commercial action of this phase. The installer channel is the route to the majority of the SAM and cannot be built quickly — starting in Phase 1 is not optional.

**What success looks like at 6 months**:
- 400–700 total customers, majority Optimizer or self-directed Delegator
- 15–20 active VVS installers, generating their first OPTi installations
- A library of 10–20 real savings and comfort case studies, segmented by heat pump type
- The comfort protection mechanism elevated as a homepage hero claim
- A consistent value order (Reassurance → Smart → Wellbeing) locked across website and sales materials

---

### 4.4 Phase 2: 6–12 Months — Scale the Delegator

**Priority segments**: The Delegator (primary scale play), The Solar Owner (specific campaign)

**The Delegator is the volume play**. Småbarnsfamiljer, Effektavgiftsdrabbade hushåll, and Passiva rörliga elkunder together represent the broadest slice of the SAM. Converting them requires a messaging shift: Reassurance-first, not Smart-first. The proof base built in Phase 1 now makes this possible.

**Campaign architecture for the Delegator**:
- Meta/Facebook targeting, 40–60 age cohort, SE3/SE4 postcodes
- 60–90 second testimonial video format: "Pågår bakom kulisserna. Vi lever precis som vanligt."
- Installer referral program with active installers
- Landing page variant: comfort and peace of mind before savings calculator

**The Solar Owner campaign**: A specific, time-limited opportunity. The 60-öre subsidy reduction is a recent and painful ROI disruption. "What to do now that the 60-öre subsidy is gone" is a high-intent content hook. Solar installer partnerships provide efficient access to a self-identified, motivated audience.

**What success looks like at 12 months**:
- 3,500–5,000 cumulative customers
- Delegator conversion rate improving (measured by landing page variant tests)
- Solar Owner acquisition sequence generating measurable results
- 40+ active VVS installers
- Comfort Keeper as identified household blocking segment — installer briefing material in development

---

### 4.5 Beyond 12 Months — Scale and Deepen

**Priority segments**: Comfort Keeper (conversion unlock), B2B channel partnerships (volume scale)

**The Comfort Keeper**: Not easily reached through digital channels. Requires in-person formats, lifestyle editorial, and installer advocacy. The Steninge seminar model needs to be productized — a repeatable event format that can be delivered in partnership with housing associations, home improvement retailers, and local energy advisors across SE3/SE4. By this phase, the Comfort Keeper conversation shifts from acquisition to household unlock: addressing the partner of a Delegator or Optimizer who is holding up a household decision.

**B2B channel partnerships — sequenced by build time**:
- **Elhandelsbolag** (white-label, churn reduction): Integration timeline is the limiting factor. Referral pilot in Year 1, activation in Year 2.
- **Solcells-/batteribolag** (bundle complement): Once Solar Owner direct channel is established, a bundle-and-referral arrangement becomes clean.
- **Försäkringsbolag** (CARE-alignment, premium bundle): Long sales cycle; best activated once Effira has 5,000+ customers and independent proof of comfort performance.
- **Retail (Elgiganten)**: Mass distribution only when the product experience is frictionless enough for a self-select retail purchase — requires installation to be close to plug-and-play.

**What success looks like beyond 12 months**:
- Installer channel generating 5,000+ installations/year
- First B2B channel partnership live (most likely Elhandelsbolag or solar company)
- Comfort Keeper acquisition integrated into installer briefing and in-person event program
- Third-party validated case study published (savings + comfort, with temperature data)
- 15,000+ cumulative customers on track by end-2028
